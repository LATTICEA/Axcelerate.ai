WildEarth - Animal Detection and Facial Recognition

The Problem

WildEarth is a British-South African broadcasting and conservation company that connects people with wildlife. One of the ways in which they do this is by streaming live safaris. WildEarth was interested in developing an animal registry database consisting of all individual animals that appear in their safaris. However, manually doing this would have required a constant effort of hundreds of hours and would not be scalable as the number of animals in the database increases.

How We Solved It

We built an AI-based computer vision system that is able to process live safari feeds in real time. The AI system automatically detects each animal that appears and compares it with all other animals in the registry database. If the animal is not found then it creates a new entry for it. Otherwise, it simply updates its location based on where the live feed is being streamed from. Knowing the last known location of different animals can really aid in wildlife conservation activities. Furthermore, the system also takes several 300x300 snapshots of each animal that appears and then saves the best one. The best snapshot is the one in which the animal is clearly visible. This is determined by first identifying different facial landmarks - e.g., eyes, nose, ears and horns - in each snapshot and then ranking them based on the total number of landmarks detected.

Impact

Our AI system was able to automatically create the entire animal registry database, thus saving thousands of manual hours that would have been spent otherwise. Keeping track of all animals also aids in conservation activities.
